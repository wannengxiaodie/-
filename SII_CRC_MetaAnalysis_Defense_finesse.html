A Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis

Prognostic Value of the Systemic Immune-Inflammation Index for Colorectal Cancer.

A pooled analysis of 35 studies and 26,812 patients clarifies where SII predicts survival, and where it does not. 系统免疫炎症指数对结直肠癌预后价值的系统综述与 Meta 分析

01

The Clinical Challenge.

临床问题与知识缺口

Colorectal cancer remains a leading cause of cancer-related mortality worldwide. TNM staging alone cannot account for the wide inter-individual variability in prognosis, and systemic inflammation is now understood to drive tumor progression, metastasis, and treatment resistance.

Existing inflammatory markers such as NLR and PLR show promise but capture only part of the inflammatory-immune axis. The Systemic Immune-Inflammation Index (SII) combines three compartments into a single composite read. Prior SII studies in CRC, however, report inconsistent findings due to methodological heterogeneity and undefined cut-off values.

SII · Composite Index
SII = Platelet × Neutrophil Lymphocyte

Knowledge Gap 知识缺口

  • Optimal SII cut-off values remain undefined across the literature.
  • Prognostic value varies by treatment modality, TNM stage, and tumor location.
  • No comprehensive meta-analysis with sufficient subgroup granularity exists.
  • The most recent prior analysis (Tan et al., 2025) was limited to 27 studies, searched to March 2024.
02

Study Objectives.

研究目标与设计

Primary Objective

To clarify the prognostic value of SII for overall survival (OS) and progression-free survival (PFS) in colorectal cancer through a comprehensive meta-analysis.

Secondary Objectives

  • Explore heterogeneity sources via subgroup analyses: TNM stage, tumor location, treatment, geography, timing of SII measurement.
  • Assess whether variability in SII cut-off value moderates prognostic estimates via meta-regression.
  • Compare findings with previous meta-analyses and provide updated evidence through November 2025.

SII & Overall Survival.

主要结果 · 总生存期
2.11HR · pooled
Elevated SII is significantly associated with worse overall survival in colorectal cancer. 95% CI 1.73 – 2.57  ·  p < 0.00001  ·  32 cohorts · 25,366 patients · I² = 92% · random-effects

Heterogeneity remains high across all subgroups, consistent with clinical rather than threshold-driven variation. Pooled estimate: HR 2.11 [1.73, 2.57]. In the full 32-cohort plot, study weights ranged from ~1% to ~6%, the largest being Gao 2022 (n = 8,384).

Biological Mechanisms.

SII 预后价值的生物学机制

Neutrophilia

中性粒细胞升高
  • Release of VEGF & MMPs
  • Promote angiogenesis
  • Facilitate metastasis
  • Remodel extracellular matrix

Thrombocytosis

血小板增多
  • Platelet–tumor aggregates
  • Protect CTCs from shear stress
  • Shield from immune surveillance
  • Enhance tumor cell survival

Lymphocytopenia

淋巴细胞减少
  • Impaired CD8+ T-cell function
  • Reduced antitumor immunity
  • Decreased immunosurveillance
  • Treg & MDSC infiltration
The combined effect of elevated neutrophils and platelets with reduced lymphocytes creates a pro-tumor microenvironment, enhances infiltration of immunosuppressive cells (MDSCs, Tregs), promotes angiogenesis, and impairs cytotoxic T-cell-mediated antitumor immunity. SII captures all three compartments in a single composite index, more comprehensive than NLR or PLR alone. 炎性-免疫失衡驱动肿瘤微环境重塑；SII 同时捕获中性粒、血小板、淋巴三个维度的复合信息，优于 NLR 或 PLR。

Clinical Implications.

讨论 · 临床意义与创新点

Proposed Clinical Framework

  • Baseline SII at diagnosis for risk stratification.
  • Serial SII monitoring (e.g., every 3 months).
  • Integrate with TNM staging, especially in Stage III.
  • Treatment intensification if SII persistently elevated.
  • Nomograms combining SII with molecular and clinical variables.

Key Clinical Insights

Stage III shows the strongest association (OS HR 3.65, PFS HR 3.64). SII may guide treatment intensification. Post-treatment > baseline (OS HR 2.44 vs 1.98) supports dynamic monitoring. Bevacizumab attenuates the signal (OS HR 1.78; PFS HR 1.41), suggesting anti-inflammatory properties. Elderly (≥65) show stronger effect (meta-regression p = 0.041), reflecting cumulative inflammatory burden.

Limitations.

研究局限性
1

Cut-off Heterogeneity

截断值异质性

SII cut-off values ranged from 340 to 1,505. Although meta-regression did not identify it as a significant moderator, this variability limits cross-study comparability and impedes standardization of clinical thresholds.

2

Study Design Imbalance

研究设计失衡

38 of 40 cohorts were retrospective; only 2 were prospective. Retrospective designs introduce potential confounders and selection bias. Prospective data for PFS did not reach significance (p = 0.07).

3

Geographic Bias

地域偏倚

The majority of studies originate from Asia (China, Japan), with limited European representation and minimal data from North America, Africa, and other regions. Findings may not generalize to non-Asian populations.

4

Tumor Location Granularity

肿瘤部位分辨率不足

Most original studies did not provide HRs stratified by right-sided vs. left-sided CRC, despite known biological differences (MSI, BRAF, microbiome) between proximal and distal tumors.

5

High Heterogeneity

高异质性

I² = 92% for OS and 73% for PFS. Despite consistent effect direction across all studies, the high heterogeneity warrants cautious interpretation and underscores the need for standardized protocols in future prospective studies.

13

Conclusions & Future Directions.

结论与未来研究方向

Conclusions 结论

  • Elevated pre-treatment SII is significantly associated with worse OS (HR 2.11) and PFS (HR 2.16) in CRC, based on 35 studies and 26,812 patients.
  • SII is a robust, accessible, and cost-effective prognostic biomarker derived from routine blood counts.
  • Prognostic value is consistent across diverse clinical subgroups; strongest in Stage III disease (OS HR 3.65).
  • Post-treatment SII may offer superior prognostic precision compared to static baseline measurements.
  • Cut-off heterogeneity and geographic bias limit immediate universal clinical implementation.

Future Research 未来方向

  • Large-scale prospective multicenter studies with standardized SII measurement protocols.
  • ROC-based validation of universally applicable optimal SII cut-off values.
  • Integration of SII with molecular subtyping (MSI, KRAS, BRAF) and other biomarkers.
  • Investigation of dynamic SII monitoring (DSII) protocols for treatment response assessment.
  • Expansion to geographically diverse populations via multi-center international collaborations.
  • Development of nomograms integrating SII with clinical and molecular variables for personalized prognostication.
