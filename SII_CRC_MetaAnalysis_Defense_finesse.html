Prognostic Value of the Systemic Immune-Inflammation Index for Colorectal Cancer.

A pooled analysis of 35 studies and 26,812 patients clarifies where SII predicts survival, and where it does not. 系统免疫炎症指数对结直肠癌预后价值的系统综述与 Meta 分析

01

The Clinical Challenge.

临床问题与知识缺口

Colorectal cancer remains a leading cause of cancer-related mortality worldwide. TNM staging alone cannot account for the wide inter-individual variability in prognosis, and systemic inflammation is now understood to drive tumor progression, metastasis, and treatment resistance.

Existing inflammatory markers such as NLR and PLR show promise but capture only part of the inflammatory-immune axis. The Systemic Immune-Inflammation Index (SII) combines three compartments into a single composite read. Prior SII studies in CRC, however, report inconsistent findings due to methodological heterogeneity and undefined cut-off values.

SII · Composite Index
SII = Platelet × Neutrophil Lymphocyte

Knowledge Gap

  • Optimal SII cut-off values remain undefined across the literature.
  • Prognostic value varies by treatment modality, TNM stage, and tumor location.
  • No comprehensive meta-analysis with sufficient subgroup granularity exists.
  • The most recent prior analysis (Tan et al., 2025) was limited to 27 studies, searched to March 2024.
02

Study Objectives.

研究目标与设计

Primary Objective

To clarify the prognostic value of SII for overall survival (OS) and progression-free survival (PFS) in colorectal cancer through a comprehensive meta-analysis.

Secondary Objectives

  • Explore heterogeneity sources via subgroup analyses: TNM stage, tumor location, treatment, geography, timing of SII measurement.
  • Assess whether variability in SII cut-off value moderates prognostic estimates via meta-regression.
  • Compare findings with previous meta-analyses and provide updated evidence through November 2025.

SII & Overall Survival.

主要结果 · 总生存期
2.11HR · pooled
Elevated SII is significantly associated with worse overall survival in colorectal cancer. 95% CI 1.73 – 2.57 · p < 0.00001 · 32 cohorts · 25,366 patients · I² = 92%

Figure 1 · Subgroup forest plot for overall survival. All subgroups show consistent direction: higher SII correlates with worse OS. Stage III shows the strongest association.

Other Moderators

Age (≥65 vs <65) emerged as a significant moderator for OS (p = 0.041), with stronger effect in elderly populations. This may reflect cumulative inflammatory burden with aging.

Publication year, sample size, and geographic region did not reach statistical significance as moderators.

Biological Mechanisms.

生物学机制

Neutrophilia

中性粒细胞升高
  • Release of VEGF & MMPs
  • Promote angiogenesis
  • Facilitate metastasis

Thrombocytosis

血小板增多
  • Platelet-tumor aggregates
  • Protect circulating tumor cells
  • Enhance tumor cell survival

Lymphocytopenia

淋巴细胞减少
  • Impaired CD8+ T-cell function
  • Reduced antitumor immunity
  • Treg & MDSC infiltration
The combined effect of elevated neutrophils and platelets with reduced lymphocytes creates a pro-tumor microenvironment. SII captures all three compartments in a single composite index. 炎性-免疫失衡驱动肿瘤微环境重塑；SII 同时捕获三个维度的复合信息。

Clinical Implications.

讨论 · 临床意义

Proposed Clinical Framework

  • Baseline SII at diagnosis for risk stratification.
  • Serial SII monitoring every 3 months.
  • Integrate with TNM staging, especially in Stage III.
  • Treatment intensification if SII persistently elevated.
Stage III shows the strongest association (OS HR 3.65). Post-treatment SII (OS HR 2.44 vs 1.98) supports dynamic monitoring. Bevacizumab attenuates the signal, suggesting anti-inflammatory properties.

13

Conclusions & Future Directions.

结论与未来方向

Conclusions

  • Elevated pre-treatment SII predicts worse OS (HR 2.11) and PFS (HR 2.16).
  • SII is a robust, accessible prognostic biomarker from routine blood counts.
  • Strongest in Stage III disease (OS HR 3.65).
  • Post-treatment SII may offer superior prognostic precision.

Future Research

  • Large-scale prospective multicenter studies with standardized protocols.
  • ROC-based validation of optimal SII cut-off values.
  • Integration with molecular subtyping (MSI, KRAS, BRAF).
  • Development of nomograms for personalized prognostication.
